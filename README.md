Medical Question Answering with PubMedQA

Literature Search Contribution

This document details the literature search contribution for our group project focused on Medical Question Answering with PubMedQA.

Overview & Objectives

As part of the project methodology, a focused literature search was conducted to identify key research papers aligned with our primary tasks. The selected papers support the core goals of the project, which include:

Understanding the structure and nuances of the PubMedQA dataset.

Comparing general-purpose language models against biomedical-specific language models.

Exploring approaches for answer generation and classification.

Reviewing broader biomedical question answering challenges and evaluation metrics.

These references were foundational in supporting the motivation, method design, and literature review sections of our final project report.

Identified Papers and Relevance

Below is the curated list of research papers selected for this project, along with a brief justification of their relevance to our work:

1. PubMedQA: A Dataset for Biomedical Research Question Answering

Authors: Qiao Jin, Bhuwan Dhingra, Zhengping Liu, William W. Cohen, Xinghua Lu.

Relevance: Directly aligned with our core task as it introduces the primary dataset (PubMedQA) used in our project.

2. Biomedical Question Answering: A Survey of Approaches and Challenges

Authors: Qiao Jin, Zheng Yuan, Guangzhi Xiong, Qianlan Yu, Huaiyuan Ying, Chuanqi Tan, Mosha Chen, Songfang Huang, Xiaozhong Liu, Sheng Yu.

Relevance: Highly useful for the introduction and literature review; summarizes major biomedical QA methods, state-of-the-art approaches, and ongoing challenges in the field.

3. BioBERT: a pre-trained biomedical language representation model for biomedical text mining

Authors: Jinhyuk Lee, Wonjin Yoon, Sungdong Kim, Donghyeon Kim, Sunkyu Kim, Chan Ho So, Jaewoo Kang.

Relevance: Essential for comparing domain-specific biomedical text representations against standard, general NLP models.

4. Domain-Specific Language Model Pretraining for Biomedical Natural Language Processing

Authors: Yu Gu, Robert Tinn, Hao Cheng, Michael Lucas, Naoto Usuyama, Xiaodong Liu, Tristan Naumann, Jianfeng Gao, Hoifung Poon.

Relevance: This is the foundational PubMedBERT paper, strongly relevant for understanding the benefits of biomedical-only pretraining from scratch.

5. ClinicalBERT: Modeling Clinical Notes and Predicting Hospital Readmission

Authors: Kexin Huang, Jaan Altosaar, Rajesh Ranganath.

Relevance: Helps justify discussions surrounding domain adaptation in medical language models, offering insights into clinical-note focused modeling.

6. SciFive: a text-to-text transformer model for biomedical literature

Authors: Long N. Phan, James T. Anibal, Hieu Tran, Shaurya Chanana, Erol Bahadroglu, Alec Peltekian, Grégoire Altan-Bonnet.

Relevance: Relevant for exploring generation-style biomedical QA and the application of text-to-text transformer modeling in this domain.

7. A Challenge on Large-Scale Biomedical Semantic Indexing and Question Answering

Authors: George Tsatsaronis, Michael Schroeder, Georgios Paliouras, Yannis Almirantis, Ion Androutsopoulos, Eric Gaussier, Patrick Gallinari, Thierry Artieres, Matthias Alvers, Matthias Zschunke, et al.

Relevance: Provides important background context by introducing BioASQ, a major and widely recognized biomedical QA benchmark.

8. Transfer Learning in Biomedical Natural Language Processing: An Evaluation of BERT and ELMo on Ten Benchmarking Datasets

Authors: Yifan Peng, Shankai Yan, Zhiyong Lu.

Relevance: Highly useful for explaining the evaluation and benchmarking of biomedical language models across various established datasets.

9. Sequence Tagging for Biomedical Extractive Question Answering

Authors: Wonjin Yoon, Richard Jackson, Aron Lagerberg, Jaewoo Kang.

Relevance: Relevant for understanding extractive biomedical QA methods and answer span modeling techniques.

10. BioReader: a Retrieval-Enhanced Text-to-Text Transformer for Biomedical Literature

Authors: Giacomo Frisoni, Miki Mizutani, Gianluca Moro, Lorenzo Valgimigli.

Relevance: Useful for discussing and analyzing retrieval-enhanced (RAG) and generation-based biomedical QA pipelines.
